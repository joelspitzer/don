Donald W. Zeigler, PhD

Public Health Advocate, Educator, and Prevention Specialist

Professional Profile

Dr. Donald Zeigler is a nationally recognized public health leader with over five decades of experience in health promotion. His career has been defined by his expertise in the intersection of global trade policy and the prevention of non-communicable diseases.

"A dedicated advocate for public health policy, focused on bridging the gap between clinical research and community wellness."

Career & Academic History

Academic Appointments

University & Medical Instruction

  • Rush University Medical Center: Assistant Professor, Community and Social Medicine, Department of Preventive Medicine.
  • University of Illinois Chicago (UIC): Adjunct Associate Professor, Health Policy and Administration, School of Public Health.
American Medical Association

AMA Leadership

Served for over 16 years in high-level leadership, including roles as Director of Prevention and Healthy Lifestyles and Director of the Office of Alcohol and Other Drug Abuse. He also served as Secretary of the AMA Council on Long Range Planning and Development.

National & International Service

Liaison & Policy Roles

  • Global Alcohol Policy Conference: Featured Speaker and contributor to international policy frameworks.
  • CDC Liaison: Served on the Community Preventive Services Task Force, evaluating national health promotion interventions.
