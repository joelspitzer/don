Donald W. Zeigler, PhD

Public Health Advocate, Educator, and Prevention Specialist

Professional Profile

Dr. Donald Zeigler is a seasoned public health professional with over five decades of experience in health promotion and disease prevention. His work has spanned international volunteerism, community hospital leadership, and national policy advocacy at the highest levels.

Career Highlights

Early 1970s

Peace Corps Brazil

Served as a volunteer in Brazil, focusing on community health and international development foundations.

1972 - 1978

Advocacy & Cessation

Held key roles at the Chicago Lung Association and the American Cancer Society, leading early initiatives in smoking cessation and respiratory health.

1978 - 2000

Skokie Valley Community Hospital

Director of the Good Health Program. Transformed community health in Skokie through proactive clinical education, screenings, and wellness workshops for over two decades.

National Policy

American Medical Association (AMA)

Director of Prevention and Healthy Lifestyles. Spearheaded national health campaigns and published influential research on neurocognitive health and global tobacco control.

Academic Service

University of Illinois Chicago (UIC)

Adjunct Associate Professor. Specialized in Health Policy and Administration, researching the intersection of global trade and public health outcomes.

Community & Board Leadership

Dr. Zeigler has served on numerous boards to shape local and national health policy:

  • Evanston Health Advisory Council (EHAC): Former Chair
  • Skokie Board of Health: Board Member
  • American Heart Association: Contributor and Board Member
  • American Public Health Association: Alcohol, Tobacco, and Other Drug Section
