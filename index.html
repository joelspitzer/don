Donald W. Zeigler, PhD

Public Health Advocate, Educator, and Prevention Specialist

Professional Profile

Dr. Donald Zeigler is a seasoned public health professional with over five decades of experience in health promotion and disease prevention. His work has spanned international volunteerism, community hospital leadership, and national policy advocacy at the highest levels.

"An expert in the intersection of global trade and public health, Dr. Zeigler has dedicated his career to the prevention of non-communicable diseases."

Career Highlights

Early 1970s

Peace Corps Brazil

Served as a volunteer in Brazil, establishing a lifelong foundation in community-based health and international development foundation.

1972 - 1978

Advocacy & Cessation

Held key roles at the Chicago Lung Association and the American Cancer Society, leading early initiatives in smoking cessation and respiratory health.

1978 - 2000

Skokie Valley Community Hospital

Director of the Good Health Program. Pioneered hospital-based wellness in Skokie, delivering clinical health education and preventative screenings directly to residents for 22 years.

National Leadership

American Medical Association (AMA)

Director of Prevention and Healthy Lifestyles. Led the Office of Alcohol, Tobacco, and Other Drug Abuse Prevention. Spearheaded research into the neurocognitive effects of alcohol and global tobacco control policy.

Academic Appointments

Academic Leadership

  • Rush University Medical Center: Assistant Professor in Community and Social Medicine within the Department of Preventive Medicine.
  • University of Illinois Chicago (UIC): Adjunct Associate Professor of Health Policy and Administration, School of Public Health.

Boards & Service

Dr. Zeigler has provided leadership to numerous health advisory bodies:

  • Evanston Health Advisory Council (EHAC): Former Chair
  • Skokie Board of Health: Long-term Board Member
  • American Heart Association: Contributor and Board Member
  • Global Trade and Health Forum: Expert contributor regarding alcohol and tobacco control.
